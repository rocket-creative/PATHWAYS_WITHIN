## The Nature of Trauma

**Many of us experience trauma after an intense experience** that may affect our mental, behavioral, and emotional wellbeing.

**Trauma can live with you for your entire life**, affecting you below the surface as well as manifesting through your personality, actions, and thoughts. Often, people report not remembering a traumatic experience clearly, but through therapy the memories start to drift backâ€”or sometimes they never do, and that's okay too.

### What Counts as Trauma?

**"Trauma" once only referred to large-scale events** like sexual assault, military combat, or car accidents. In recent years, we've come to understand that trauma has a much more widespread effect than this.

**We now recognize:**

**Big-T Trauma (single events)**
- Sexual assault or abuse
- Physical violence
- Serious accidents
- Natural disasters
- Combat exposure
- Medical trauma
- Sudden loss of a loved one
- Witnessing violence

**Little-t Trauma (cumulative experiences)**
- Childhood emotional neglect
- Bullying or humiliation
- Divorce or family conflict
- Emotional abuse
- Betrayal or abandonment
- Chronic invalidation
- Growing up in an unsafe environment

**Complex Trauma (ongoing/repeated)**
- Childhood abuse (physical, sexual, emotional)
- Domestic violence relationships
- Long-term captivity or trafficking
- Repeated medical procedures (especially in childhood)
- Growing up with addicted or mentally ill parents
- Institutional abuse

**The accumulation of smaller moments where we felt unsafe and unprotected** creates what we call complex traumaâ€”and it's just as valid and impactful as single-incident trauma.

## How Trauma Affects You

**Trauma isn't just "in your head"â€”it affects your entire being.**

### Mental & Emotional Effects

**Intrusive Memories**
- Flashbacks (feeling like it's happening again)
- Nightmares and night terrors
- Unwanted thoughts or images
- Inability to stop thinking about it

**Avoidance**
- Avoiding people, places, or situations that remind you
- Avoiding talking or thinking about what happened
- Emotional numbing
- Feeling detached from life

**Negative Changes in Thinking**
- Negative beliefs about yourself ("I'm damaged," "It was my fault")
- Negative beliefs about others ("No one is trustworthy")
- Negative beliefs about the world ("Nowhere is safe")
- Difficulty trusting
- Persistent shame, guilt, or fear
- Loss of interest in things that once mattered

**Hyperarousal (Always on Alert)**
- Being easily startled
- Feeling on edge or tense
- Difficulty sleeping
- Irritability or anger outbursts
- Difficulty concentrating
- Hypervigilance (constantly scanning for danger)

### Physical Effects

**Your body remembers what your mind might forget:**
- Chronic pain (headaches, body aches)
- Digestive issues
- Autoimmune conditions
- Sleep disturbances
- Fatigue
- Tension (tight shoulders, jaw clenching)
- Sexual difficulties
- Chronic illness

### Behavioral Effects

**Trauma often leads to:**
- Substance use (self-medication)
- Self-harm behaviors
- Eating disorders
- Risky behaviors
- Difficulty maintaining relationships
- Work or school problems
- Social isolation

### Identify Triggers

**Triggers are reminders of trauma** that activate your stress response even when you're not in danger.

**Common triggers:**
- Sensory (smells, sounds, sights)
- Situational (similar places or circumstances)
- Interpersonal (relationship dynamics)
- Emotional (certain feelings)
- Dates (anniversaries of traumatic events)

**In therapy, you'll:**
- Identify what specifically triggers you
- Understand why these things are triggering
- Learn to manage triggers when they arise
- Gradually reduce the power triggers have over you

**Safety is the foundation of trauma recovery.**

**We'll help you create:**
- Physical safety (in your environment and relationships)

- Internal safety (feeling safe in your own body)

**Safety work includes:**

- Developing self-soothing skills
- Learning to trust your instincts

## Therapeutic Approaches for Trauma

### Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT)

**CPT aims to heal the harm caused by traumatic experience** by examining and challenging unhelpful beliefs.

**How it works:**
- Identify "stuck points" (beliefs keeping you trapped)
- Examine evidence for and against these beliefs
- Develop more balanced, accurate thoughts
- Change how you relate to what happened

**Example stuck points:**
- "It was my fault" â†’ "I did the best I could in an impossible situation"
- "I can't trust anyone" â†’ "Some people are trustworthy; I can learn to recognize them"
- "I'm damaged forever" â†’ "I was hurt, but I'm healing and growing"

**CPT helps you "rewire" how your brain reacts** to thoughts about the trauma.

**For many patients, CPT has helped them:**
- Move past stuck points
- Develop healthy coping skills
- Manage existing trauma
- Build resilience for the future

---

### EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing)

**EMDR is a structured approach** that uses bilateral stimulation to help your brain process traumatic memories.

**What makes it different:**
- More structured than talk therapy
- Involves specific activities (eye movements, tapping, sounds)
- Helps resolve trauma without extensive verbal processing
- Works directly with how memories are stored

**The process:**
1. Identify traumatic memory
2. Notice body sensations and emotions
3. Follow bilateral stimulation (eye movements, tapping)
4. Allow brain to process and reprocess
5. Memory becomes less disturbing

**Benefits:**
- Don't have to describe trauma in detail
- Relatively quick (often 6-12 sessions)
- Addresses multiple traumas
- Strong research support

**EMDR is a form of neurophysiological therapy** that helps clients resolve issues without having to talk extensively about them.

---

### Somatic Therapy

**Trauma lives in your body**, and somatic therapy addresses this directly.

**Body-centered approaches include:**
- Noticing body sensations
- Releasing trapped tension
- Completing interrupted survival responses
- Restoring sense of safety in your body
- Grounding techniques

**Why this matters:**
Your body holds trauma even when your mind doesn't remember. Addressing the physical component is often essential for complete healing.

---

### Traditional Talk Therapy

**Sometimes you need to tell your story** and have it witnessed by someone who truly understands.

**Benefits:**
- Build relationship with therapist
- Feel heard and validated
- Make sense of what happened
- Explore emotions safely
- Gain insights and perspective

**Combined with other approaches**, talk therapy provides comprehensive support.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I know if I have trauma?

**Trauma is often followed by a series of symptoms:**
- Unwanted memories or flashbacks
- Avoidance of people or situations
- Mental and emotional distress
- Problems remembering the traumatic experience
- Feeling anger or irritability
- Negative self-image
- Difficulty trusting
- Feeling unsafe in the world
- Physical symptoms without clear cause

**If these symptoms are interfering with your life**, trauma therapy can helpâ€”whether you have a clear traumatic event in mind or not.

**You don't need a diagnosis** to deserve support.

---

### EMDR sounds interesting but I'm afraid of losing control. Is it right for me?

**This is a very common concern.**

**The truth about EMDR:**
- You remain fully in control throughout
- You can stop at any time
- You don't lose consciousness or awareness
- You won't be forced to confront anything you're not ready for
- Your therapist guides the process safely

**If you're experiencing trauma symptoms**, EMDR might be worth trying. EMDR is definitely not your normal talk therapy, but that's what makes it so effective.

**We would have a thorough conversation** about what it entails, answer all your questions, and you would make the decision when you feel ready.

### Do I have to talk about my trauma?

**Of course not.**

**While talking can be beneficial** to both yourself and your therapist (they get to know your story better), we understand that trauma is serious and we would never force you to discuss something you're not comfortable sharing.

**Some therapies (like EMDR and somatic therapy)** don't require detailed verbal processing.

**However, we also want to say this:** It can be incredibly empowering to own your story and reshape the narrative as you see fit. When you begin to talk about what happened to you (when and if you're ready), the trauma often loses some of its power over you, and you're able to resume control.

**You decide when and how much to share.** We meet you where you are.

---

### Will I ever "get over" my trauma?

**Trauma becomes part of your story**, but it doesn't have to define your story.

**Realistic goals:**
- Symptoms significantly decrease or resolve
- Triggers lose their power
- You can remember without reliving
- The past feels like the past
- You reclaim your life and joy
- You develop resilience and strength

**Complete erasure isn't usually the goal or realistic.** Instead, trauma becomes integratedâ€”something that happened to you that you've learned from and healed from, rather than something that's actively harming you every day.

**Many trauma survivors report that healing:**
- Transformed them in positive ways
- Gave them strength they didn't know they had
- Deepened their compassion and empathy
- Clarified their values and priorities
- Connected them to meaning and purpose

**Trauma resolved truly can be a gift**â€”not because trauma is good, but because the healing journey reveals your resilience.

---

### How long does trauma therapy take?

**It varies significantly based on:**
- Type and severity of trauma
- How long ago it occurred
- Your support system
- Your readiness to engage
- The therapeutic approach used

**General timelines:**

**Single-incident trauma with EMDR:** Sometimes 6-12 sessions

**Complex trauma:** Often 1-2+ years of consistent work

**Ongoing support:** Some people benefit from long-term therapy

**There's no "right" timeline.** Healing happens at the pace it needs to happen.
