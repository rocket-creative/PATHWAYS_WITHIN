## Wellness + Therapy Integration (HIPAA/FTC Compliant)

---

## OVERVIEW

Pathways Within operates two interconnected brands:

**Pathways Within Therapy** (5 locations + telehealth)
- Mental health services under licensed clinical supervision

**Pathways Within Wellness** (2 locations: Massapequa + Garden City)
- Spa and wellness services

**Ownership Disclosure:** Both practices operate under common ownership. This must be disclosed in all cross-promotional materials.

## PART 1: COMPLIANT CROSS-PROMOTION PRINCIPLES

### What We CAN Do

1. **General website mentions** of sister practice existing (not targeted to individuals)
2. **Face-to-face mentions** during appointments (provider discretion)
3. **Opt-in marketing** with explicit HIPAA-compliant authorization
4. **Educational content** about mind-body wellness (no outcome claims)
5. **Shared location information** (noting practices share buildings)
6. **Brochure availability** at each location

### What We CANNOT Do

1. âŒ Use client/patient lists for cross-marketing without authorization
2. âŒ Send automated marketing based on visit history
3. âŒ Make claims about treatment outcomes
4. âŒ Imply diagnosis or suggest someone "needs" the other service
5. âŒ Claim services "accelerate," "enhance," or "improve" each other's outcomes
6. âŒ Create "packages" bundling therapy with wellness
7. âŒ Use clinical language to justify commercial cross-sells

## PART 4: FACE-TO-FACE MENTION GUIDELINES

### For Therapy Providers

**When appropriate to mention (provider discretion):**
- Client asks about stress relief, relaxation, or physical tension
- Client mentions interest in massage, acupuncture, etc.
- Natural conversation where it's relevant

**Script:**
> "I want to mention that we have a sister practice called Pathways Within 
> Wellness that offers massage and other services. It's completely separate 
> from your therapy and totally optional. Here's their card if you're ever 
> interested."

**Do NOT say:**
- "I think you should try massage, it would help your therapy"
- "Massage can help release what we're working on"
- "Many of my clients find it helps with their anxiety"
- Anything implying clinical benefit or recommendation

### For Wellness Providers

**When appropriate to mention (provider discretion):**
- Client mentions stress, difficult life circumstances, relationship issues
- Client asks about mental health support
- Natural conversation where it's relevant

**Script:**
> "I want to mention that we have a sister practice called Pathways Within 
> Therapy if you're ever interested in that kind of support. It's completely 
> separate from your wellness services. Here's their card."

**Do NOT say:**
- "It sounds like you need therapy"
- "Your tension might be from unresolved trauma"
- "A therapist could help you work through what's causing this"
- Anything implying diagnosis or clinical assessment
