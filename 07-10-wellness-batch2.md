## Trigger Point Injections

**Trigger point injections are a minimally invasive treatment** that can provide rapid relief from chronic muscle pain and tension.

**What are trigger points?**  
Tight, irritable "knots" in muscles that cause local pain and refer pain to other areas. For example, a trigger point in your shoulder might cause headaches.

**How it works:**  
A small amount of medication (often a local anesthetic, sometimes with a corticosteroid) is injected directly into the affected muscle trigger point, helping to release the knot, reduce inflammation, and interrupt the pain cycle.

**Benefits:**
- Quick relief from muscle pain (often within minutes to hours)
- Minimal downtime (resume normal activities same day)
- Can be used in combination with other treatments
- Long-lasting results (weeks to months)
- Helps break pain cycles

**Common areas treated:**
- Neck and shoulders
- Upper and lower back
- Jaw (TMJ)
- Hips and glutes
- Legs

**Treatment frequency:** Varies based on response, typically every 4-12 weeks as needed.

## What is Laser Hair Removal?

**Laser hair removal is a cosmetic procedure** that uses concentrated beams of light to target and destroy hair follicles, preventing future hair growth.

**It's:**
- Effective (80-90% permanent reduction)
- Safe (FDA-approved)
- Long-lasting (results last years)
- Convenient (minimal maintenance)

---

## How Does It Work?

**The science:**  
Laser devices emit concentrated light energy that is absorbed by the pigment (melanin) in hair follicles. This energy heats and destroys the follicle, preventing it from producing new hair.

**The process:**
1. Laser targets dark pigment in hair
2. Light energy converts to heat
3. Heat damages hair follicle
4. Follicle can no longer produce hair
5. Existing hair falls out within 1-2 weeks

**Hair grows in cycles**, so multiple treatments are needed to catch follicles in the active growth phase.

---

**Versatile**

## Treatment Process

**Number of sessions:** 6-8 sessions typically needed  
**Spacing:** 4-6 weeks apart (varies by area)  
**Duration:** 15-60 minutes per session (depends on area size)

**Why multiple sessions?**  
Hair grows in three phases: growth (anagen), transition (catagen), and resting (telogen). Laser only works on hair in active growth phase, which is why multiple treatments are needed to catch all follicles over time.

**Why shave but not wax?**  
Laser targets the hair follicle beneath the skin. Shaving leaves the follicle intact while removing surface hair. Waxing removes the entire follicle, leaving nothing for the laser to target.
