**Best for:**

- Improving overall circulation

**What to expect:**  
Your therapist uses five basic strokesâ€”effleurage (long gliding), petrissage (kneading), friction (deep circular), tapotement (rhythmic tapping), and vibrationâ€”to create a soothing, rhythmic experience.

**Perfect when you want to:**

### Deep Tissue Massage

**A more intense massage that targets deeper layers of muscle tissue.**

**Best for:**
- Chronic pain conditions

- Injury recovery

**What to expect:**

**Communication is key**â€”always tell your therapist if pressure is too intense.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long should my massage be?

**A typical massage session is 60 minutes**, which provides enough time to address multiple areas and create meaningful relaxation.

**We also offer:**
- **30-minute express sessions** - Perfect for targeting one specific area (shoulders, back, feet) or when you're short on time
- **90-minute deep-dive sessions** - Ideal for full-body work, addressing multiple concerns, or deeply relaxing experiences

Your therapist can help you determine which duration best suits your needs and goals.

---

### How often should I get a massage?

**This depends on your individual needs and goals.**

**For stress relief and wellness maintenance:**  
Monthly massages help you manage stress and maintain wellbeing.

**For chronic pain or specific conditions:**  
Weekly or bi-weekly sessions may be needed initially, then spacing out as symptoms improve.

**For athletic performance:**  
Many athletes schedule weekly massages during heavy training periods.

**Our therapists can recommend a schedule that works best for you** based on your concerns, goals, and budget.

---

### Is massage safe?

**Yes, massage is a safe and effective therapy for most people.**

**However, certain conditions require caution or modification:**
- Blood clots or deep vein thrombosis
- Fractures or severe osteoporosis
- Burns or open wounds
- Severe bleeding disorders
- Certain cancers (check with your oncologist)
- Acute infections or fever
- Recent surgery

**We recommend consulting with your healthcare provider** if you have any health concerns before scheduling a massage.

**Always inform your therapist about:**
- Medical conditions
- Medications you're taking
- Areas to avoid (injuries, sensitive spots)
- Pregnancy
- Recent surgeries

**Before your massage:**

### Do I need to do anything to prepare?

**To get the most from your massage:**

**Before:**
- Avoid eating a large meal right before (you'll be lying on your stomach)
- Arrive a few minutes early so you're not rushed
- Use the restroom beforehand
- Remove jewelry that might get in the way
- Try to arrive in a relaxed state of mind

**After:**
- Drink plenty of water to help flush out toxins released during massage
- Avoid strenuous activity for at least 24 hours
- Take a warm bath if you'd like
- Rest and relax as much as possible
- Notice how your body feels over the next few days
