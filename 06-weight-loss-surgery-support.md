## More Than Physical Transformation

Weight loss surgery comes with many considerations, and **an often overlooked aspect is the psychological impact** of this life changing procedure. By working with a professional who understands the unique challenges of bariatric surgery, and connecting with others who have walked this path, you can better prepare yourself for the road ahead and significantly increase your chances of long term success.

Whether you're still deciding, preparing for surgery, or navigating life post op, we're here to support every step of your journey.

---

## Education and Validation

### Understanding What You're Facing

**Whether or not you've already made the decision** to have weight loss surgery, it's crucial to learn as much as you can about the procedure and its effects before moving forward.

**Weight loss surgery isn't just about physical recovery.** These procedures profoundly affect your mental health, your relationship with food, your body image, your identity, and your emotional wellbeing. Having professional support before, during, and after surgery helps you navigate these complex changes.

### Validating Your Decision

**This is a big decision**, and it makes complete sense that you have questions. You might be asking yourself:
- Am I going to be okay?
- Am I doing the right thing?
- Is the surgery worth the risks?
- Will I be able to maintain the weight loss?
- How will this change my life?
- What if I fail?

**These questions are deeply personal**, and the answers vary for each individual. That's why we encourage you to reach out for professional guidance to explore these questions in a supportive, non judgmental environment.

## Before and After: Comprehensive Support

### Pre Operative Support

**Before surgery, we offer a comprehensive psychological evaluation.** This isn't meant to make you feel inadequate or "crazy"â€”quite the opposite.

**The goals of pre op evaluation:**
- **Understand your relationship with food** and eating patterns
- **Identify underlying mental health concerns** that could affect success (depression, anxiety, eating disorders, trauma)
- **Assess readiness** for the lifestyle changes required
- **Develop strategies** to support you through recovery
- **Prepare mentally and emotionally** for the changes ahead

**Why this matters:** Nobody wants to undergo major surgery only to regain the weight. Research shows that addressing psychological factors BEFORE surgery significantly improves long term outcomes. This evaluation is a protective measure designed to help you succeed.

**We'll explore:**
- Your history with weight and dieting
- Current eating patterns and triggers
- Emotional eating or food as coping mechanism
- Support systems available to you
- Expectations for surgery and life after
- Mental health history
- Substance use patterns
- Trauma history (especially related to body, food, or abuse)

### Post Operative Support

**After surgery, the real work begins.** Your body has changed, but your brain, emotions, and habits need time to catch up.

**Post op support focuses on:**

**Adapting to Your New Body**
- Processing the rapid physical changes
- Navigating others' reactions to your weight loss
- Dealing with excess skin and body image concerns
- Adjusting to attention (positive or negative) about your appearance

**Creating New Habits**
- Learning to eat mindfully with a smaller stomach
- Recognizing hunger vs. emotional needs
- Developing non food coping strategies
- Building sustainable exercise routines

**Managing Emotions**
- Processing the loss of food as comfort
- Dealing with "transfer addiction" (substituting food with alcohol, shopping, etc.)
- Working through identity changes
- Addressing relationship shifts as you change

**Celebrating Successes**
- Acknowledging non scale victories
- Maintaining motivation through plateaus
- Building confidence in your new capabilities
- Creating a life that supports your new health

## Common Psychological Challenges After Weight Loss Surgery

### Loss of Food as Comfort

**For many people, food has been a primary coping mechanism** for stress, sadness, boredom, loneliness, or anxiety. After surgery, when you physically can't eat the same way, you lose that coping tool.

**This can feel like grief.** Working with a therapist helps you:
- Identify what emotions you were managing with food
- Develop healthier coping strategies
- Process the loss of that comfort
- Build new ways to nurture yourself

### Identity Changes

**"If I'm not the fat friend, who am I?"**

Weight loss surgery doesn't just change your bodyâ€”it can fundamentally shift how you see yourself and how others see you. This can be disorienting, exciting, scary, or all of the above.

**We help you:**
- Navigate your evolving identity
- Deal with changing relationship dynamics
- Process others' reactions (support, jealousy, sabotage)
- Build confidence in your new self

### Transfer Addiction

**When food is no longer available as a coping mechanism**, some people unconsciously transfer that addictive behavior to other substances or activitiesâ€”alcohol, shopping, gambling, or even excessive exercise.

**We watch for warning signs and help you:**
- Recognize transfer addiction early
- Address underlying issues driving addictive patterns
- Build healthy coping mechanisms
- Develop self awareness about your behavior

### Relationship Strain

**Weight loss can strain relationships in unexpected ways:**
- Partners may feel threatened by your changes
- Friends may sabotage (consciously or not) your efforts
- Family dynamics shift as you set new boundaries
- Romantic attention may increase, creating tension

**Therapy helps you navigate these challenges** while maintaining relationships that support your health.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### "My doctor answered all my questions. Why do I need therapy too?"

**Your surgeon is an expert in the physical procedure**â€”how to reshape your stomach, manage surgical risks, and monitor your physical recovery.

**A therapist is an expert in the psychological aspects**â€”how to change your relationship with food, manage emotions without eating, navigate identity shifts, and maintain long term success.

**Most surgeons require a psychological evaluation** because they understand that surgery alone isn't enough. Everyone involved wants this to be a success for you, and addressing the mental and emotional components is essential.

**Beyond the evaluation, ongoing therapy and group support help you:**
- Process the intense changes you're experiencing
- Develop skills to maintain your weight loss long term
- Address challenges as they arise
- Stay accountable and motivated

### "What are the benefits of joining the support group besides meeting others?"

**Beyond connection with people who understand, we provide:**

**Practical Resources**
- Recipes and meal ideas for post surgery eating
- Exercise suggestions and accountability
- Product recommendations (vitamins, protein options, etc.)
- Tips for navigating social situations and holidays

**Emotional Support**
- A judgment free space to share struggles
- Validation that your challenges are normal
- Hope from seeing others succeed
- Accountability to stay on track

**Expert Guidance**
- Professional facilitation to keep discussions productive
- Evidence based information about weight loss surgery
- Resources for additional support when needed

**Proof That You Matter**
- Regular connection and accountability
- Celebration of your victories
- Support through your setbacks
- Community that cares about your success

### "Can I bring a family member or friend to support group?"

**Absolutely!** We welcome support people to attend with you.

**Bringing someone can provide:**
- Support and comfort if you're nervous about attending alone
- Education for your loved one about what you're experiencing
- Better understanding between you and your support person
- Opportunity for them to connect with other support people

**Our goal is always for you to feel comfortable and safe**, and if bringing someone helps you show up and participate, we fully support that.
